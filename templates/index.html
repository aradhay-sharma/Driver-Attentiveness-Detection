Driver Attentiveness Detection
Stay Alert, Accidents Hurt.

Introduction

Staying attentive while driving, free from all distractions is a task itself, pair that up with the comfort of futuristic technology of autonomous driving that is being brought to us and that makes up the perfect recipe for a disaster to occur.
Distracted driving reduces the driver’s response time drastically May it be manual or autonomous driving.
Distraction and fatigue are visual characteristics that can be observed in their facial features like eyes and mouth.

Problem

Current studies elaborate on how autonomous vehicular crashes seem to indicate complacency which is not typically an element of conventional driving.
Autonomous vehicles seem to encourage complacency and ignorance among us drivers who are supposed to serve as the backup for these technologies.
In 2020 sleep deprived, fatigued, and distracted drivers made up for about 40% of all road accidents taking place.

Proposed System

Distraction is shown whenever a driver has reduced alertness which is displayed by gazing for a while, yawning and sluggish facial expressions.
We are proposing a Real-time image-based fatigue and attention monitoring system with the objective to decrease the consequences of inattentive driving.
A driver support system to be used as a countermeasure is required that intervenes while alerting the driver in the case of inattentive driving especially in autonomous vehicles as well as manual vehicles in order to reduce the risk of accidents and prevent crashes.



Overview



The user or driver can first initialize this system which has a quite easy setup.
Once the system starts it focuses on the drivers face and continuously captures live video feed from a camera.
It extracts and analyses various eye and mouth features.
In case an abnormality is detected in these features of the driver.
This means that the driver is inattentive and they are immediately alerted.
